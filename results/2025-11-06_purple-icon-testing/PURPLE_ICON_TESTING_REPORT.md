## Executive Summary

### Test Overview

We conducted isolated interviews with 25 synthetic personas across 5 demographic segments to evaluate the proposed PURPLE app icon for Yes Games. Each persona was interviewed independently to prevent context pollution and ensure authentic responses.

### Overall Rating: **4.24/5**

The purple app icon concept receives **strong positive reception** across ALL demographics with NO significant concerns. This is the **highest-rated color tested** (Yellow: 3.4/5, Red: 2.6/5, Purple: 4.24/5).

### Key Insight

**PURPLE delivers exceptional visibility, premium perception, AND trust—solving the fundamental challenge that both yellow and red failed to address.**

## Implementation Guide

### Phase 1: Shade Selection & Design (Week 1)

#### Recommended Purple Shades

**Option 1: Deep Royal Purple (Recommended)**
- Hex: #5D3A7D
- RGB: 93, 58, 125
- Why: Balances richness with energy. Premium but not dark. Sophisticated but not stuffy.

**Option 2: Rich Purple**
- Hex: #6A0DAD
- RGB: 106, 13, 173
- Why: More vibrant, slightly more energetic. Good for younger-skewing demographics.

**Option 3: Elegant Purple**
- Hex: #7B3F8D
- RGB: 123, 63, 141
- Why: Softer, more approachable. Good for balanced positioning.

**Avoid:**
- Bright neon purple (#BF00FF) - too aggressive, loses premium perception
- Pale lavender (#E6E6FA) - too weak, loses energy and visibility
- Muddy purple (#614051) - too dark, loses visibility and excitement

#### Typography Pairing

**For "YES" Logotype:**
- Option 1: White (#FFFFFF) for maximum contrast and clarity
- Option 2: Light gold (#F4C430) for luxury positioning
- Option 3: Light cream (#FFF8DC) for sophisticated warmth

**Font Style:**
- Bold, confident sans-serif (e.g., Montserrat Bold, Proxima Nova Bold)
- Clean, modern, professional
- High contrast ratio for accessibility (WCAG AAA)

#### Testing Requirements
- Test across device types: iPhone light mode, iPhone dark mode, Android
- Test on various background colors
- Validate contrast ratios meet accessibility standards
- A/B test 3 purple shade options with real users (optional but recommended)

### Phase 2: Brand System Integration (Week 2)

#### In-App Color System

**Primary:** Purple (chosen shade from Phase 1)
- App icon background
- Primary navigation elements
- Key CTAs (deposit, play, bet)
- Premium features indicators

**Secondary:** Dark neutral + accents
- Background: Deep charcoal (#2C2C2C) or navy (#1A1F3A)
- Accents: Gold (#FFD700) for premium elements
- Accents: White (#FFFFFF) for clarity
- Trust sections: Use dark blue or charcoal backgrounds with white text

**Trust Signal Colors:**
- Licensing badges: Keep regulatory colors (Spillemyndigheden standards)
- Responsible gambling: Use green (#4ADE80) for positive reinforcement
- Warnings (if needed): Use orange (#FF9500), NOT red
- Success states: Green (#22C55E)
- Error states: Orange (#FF9500), NOT red

#### Consistency Requirements
1. Purple must appear consistently throughout app (not just icon)
2. Premium feel must extend to all touchpoints
3. Trust signals must be visible and prominent
4. Responsible gambling tools must be easily accessible
