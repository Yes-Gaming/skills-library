# User Research Interview: App Icon Design Concept
## Yes Games - Blue Background Icon Testing

**Persona:** Lars (Variation 4.3 - Skeptical/Critical Style)
**Age:** 44
**Location:** Odense, Seden
**Date:** November 6, 2025
**Concept Tested:** Blue app icon with YES logotype

---

## Interview Questions & Responses

**Q1: What's your first reaction when you see a blue app icon on your iPhone home screen?**

Blue is the color that every company uses when they want to signal trustworthiness. From a security perspective, that makes me both more and less comfortable. More comfortable because blue suggests they understand they need to earn trust. Less comfortable because I know that bad actors also use blue precisely because of those associations.

So my first reaction is: "Okay, they're trying to look trustworthy. Now let me dig into whether they actually are."

---

**Q2: How does blue make you feel in the context of a gaming/entertainment app?**

From a security and trust standpoint, blue is appropriate. It signals that the platform is taking security seriously, which is critical when you're dealing with financial transactions and personal data. I appreciate that they're not using manipulative, dopamine-trigger colors.

However, I'm also aware that blue is surface-level signaling. What really matters is what's underneath—encryption standards, data protection policies, licensing, payout reliability. Blue is a good start, but it's just the first layer. The icon color alone doesn't make a platform secure; the actual security practices do.

That said, if I had to choose between a flashy red/yellow gambling app and a blue one, I'd investigate the blue one first because it suggests they're at least thinking about trust and legitimacy.

---

**Q3: Would blue help you find and recognize the app quickly among your other apps?**

From a pure visual recognition standpoint, no—blue is extremely common. But here's the thing: I'm not looking for instant gratification or impulsive access to gambling. The fact that blue blends in somewhat actually reinforces that this is a considered, deliberate activity rather than an impulse behavior.

If the app were bright yellow or red and instantly grabbed my attention every time I unlocked my phone, that would actually concern me from an addiction and responsible gambling perspective. Blue's calm presence is actually more aligned with responsible gambling behavior.

---

**Q4: Does blue give you any specific associations or feelings (energy, fun, caution, cheap, premium, trust, etc.)?**

Blue is strongly associated with trust, security, and stability—exactly the associations you want for a platform handling financial transactions. From a security professional's perspective, blue signals that the company understands the importance of user trust and is attempting to align with that value.

Premium vs. cheap depends on execution. A deep, saturated blue with clean design can feel very premium—think financial institutions or tech companies. A washed-out or poorly executed blue would feel generic and cheap.

The key is that blue is the right starting point for a gambling platform that wants to be taken seriously from a security and trust perspective. But it's not sufficient on its own—the actual practices must back it up.

---

**Q5: On a scale of 1-5, how much do you like the idea of a blue app icon? (1=hate it, 5=love it)**

4. From a security and trust perspective, blue is a strong choice. It signals the right values and creates the right first impression for a platform that needs to earn user trust. It's not a 5 only because I know that color alone doesn't make a platform trustworthy—the underlying security architecture, licensing, and practices matter far more.

But as a first impression and positioning statement, blue is appropriate and signals that Yes Games is serious about trust and security.

---

**Q6: Any other thoughts about blue as our brand color for the app icon?**

If Yes Games goes with blue, they need to ensure that everything behind that icon lives up to what blue promises: robust security, transparent practices, clear licensing, strong responsible gambling tools, and reliable customer service. Blue creates an expectation of trustworthiness—and if the actual experience doesn't match that expectation, users like me will feel betrayed and potentially spread negative word-of-mouth.

Blue is the right choice if—and only if—Yes Games is genuinely committed to being a trustworthy, secure, professionally run platform. If it's just surface-level branding, users will see through it. But if the commitment is real, blue is the perfect visual representation of those values.
