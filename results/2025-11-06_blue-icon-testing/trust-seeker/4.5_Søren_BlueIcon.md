# User Research Interview: App Icon Design Concept
## Yes Games - Blue Background Icon Testing

**Persona:** Søren (Variation 4.5 - Analytical/Comparative Style)
**Age:** 51
**Location:** Copenhagen, Hellerup
**Date:** November 6, 2025
**Concept Tested:** Blue app icon with YES logotype

---

## Interview Questions & Responses

**Q1: What's your first reaction when you see a blue app icon on your iPhone home screen?**

When I compare blue to the broader gambling app landscape, it's a clear positioning statement toward trust and legitimacy. Major regulated operators like bet365, William Hill, and several European platforms use blue precisely because it signals regulatory compliance and financial security. My first reaction is that Yes Games is positioning itself in that tier of trusted operators rather than as a casino-entertainment app.

---

**Q2: How does blue make you feel in the context of a gaming/entertainment app?**

From an analytical standpoint, blue addresses the primary concern that my demographic has with gambling platforms: trust and security. According to industry research, trust is the number one barrier to entry for users over 40, and blue directly mitigates that concern.

Compared to other platforms I've evaluated, blue signals several key attributes:
1. Regulatory compliance and licensing
2. Financial security and reliable payouts
3. Professional operations and customer service
4. Responsible gambling commitment

These are precisely the factors I analyze when comparing platforms. Blue tells me that Yes Games understands what mature, high-value customers prioritize.

---

**Q3: Would blue help you find and recognize the app quickly among your other apps?**

If we analyze this from a pure recognition efficiency perspective, blue has high frequency and therefore lower distinctiveness. However, I'd argue that recognition speed is the wrong metric to optimize for with my demographic.

What matters more is association quality. When I see blue apps on my home screen, I associate them with trusted, essential services—banking, financial management, professional tools. Having Yes Games sit in that visual category is actually strategic positioning, not a recognition problem.

The data shows that high-value, low-churn customers don't need instant-access gambling apps. We're deliberate users who open the app intentionally, not impulsively. Blue aligns with that usage pattern.

---

**Q4: Does blue give you any specific associations or feelings (energy, fun, caution, cheap, premium, trust, etc.)?**

Looking at the data on color psychology and brand perception:

**Trust metrics:**
- Blue scores 85-90% on trust association
- Significantly outperforms all other colors on security perception
- Highest correlation with financial services and regulated industries

**Premium positioning:**
- Deep blue (navy, cobalt) scores 70-75% on premium perception
- Comparable to black and gold in luxury associations
- Far superior to yellow, orange, or bright colors in perceived quality

**Energy/excitement:**
- Blue scores low (20-25%) on excitement metrics
- This is a feature, not a bug, for mature demographics who view gambling as calculated entertainment, not impulsive behavior

The analytical conclusion is that blue optimizes for the right metrics if Yes Games is targeting users with high lifetime value, low churn, and trust-driven decision making.

---

**Q5: On a scale of 1-5, how much do you like the idea of a blue app icon? (1=hate it, 5=love it)**

5. From a strategic and analytical perspective, blue is the optimal choice for targeting the high-value demographic that Yes Games should prioritize.

The data is clear: users over 40 with disposable income and lower churn rates—the most profitable segment in gambling—prioritize trust over excitement. Blue directly addresses their primary concern while positioning Yes Games alongside established, regulated operators.

Younger users who want excitement and energy might rate blue lower, but those users also have lower lifetime value and higher churn. Blue is making the correct strategic trade-off.

---

**Q6: Any other thoughts about blue as our brand color for the app icon?**

The strategic insight here is that blue is a market segmentation choice. By choosing blue, Yes Games is explicitly targeting:
- Age 40+ users (highest LTV)
- Trust-driven decision makers
- Calculated gamblers vs. impulsive bettors
- Users seeking regulated, professional platforms

This is the correct business decision if the financial model depends on user lifetime value rather than volume. The analysis shows that trust-seeking users have:
- 3-4x higher LTV than excitement-seeking users
- 40-50% lower churn rates
- Higher average transaction values
- Better responsible gambling behavior (lower regulatory risk)

Blue sacrifices some volume appeal to younger demographics, but that's a worthwhile trade-off for superior unit economics. If I were advising Yes Games from a financial perspective, I would strongly recommend blue as the strategic positioning choice.

The only requirement is that execution must be premium—deep, rich blue tones with sophisticated design. A cheap-looking blue would undermine the trust promise. But if executed well, blue is the financially optimal choice for sustainable, profitable growth.
