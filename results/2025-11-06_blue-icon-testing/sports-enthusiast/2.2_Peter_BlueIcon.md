# User Research Interview: App Icon Design Concept
## Yes Games - Blue Background Icon Testing

**Persona:** Peter (Variation 2.2 - Detailed/Explanatory Style)
**Age:** 32
**Location:** Aarhus, Brabrand
**Date:** November 6, 2025
**Concept Tested:** Blue app icon with YES logotype

---

## Interview Questions & Responses

**Q1: What's your first reaction when you see a blue app icon on your iPhone home screen?**

The way I see it, blue immediately positions the app in a more traditional, institutional space. When I think about sports betting apps, many of them do use blue—bet365, William Hill, several others—so there's already a category association there. My first reaction is that this feels like a mature, established platform rather than a startup or disruptor. Blue signals "we've been around, we're legitimate."

---

**Q2: How does blue make you feel in the context of a gaming/entertainment app?**

In the context of sports betting specifically, blue feels appropriate because it aligns with the analytical, strategic nature of how I approach betting. I'm not here for pure entertainment—I'm here to analyze odds, evaluate statistics, and make informed decisions. Blue feels like it respects that approach. It says "we take this seriously" rather than "this is just for fun."

However, if you analyze it from an emotional engagement perspective, blue doesn't create the same excitement that you might get from warmer colors. When you're watching a live match and you want to place a quick bet, you're not necessarily looking for calm and professional—you want responsive, immediate, energetic. So there's a tension there between signaling trust and signaling excitement.

---

**Q3: Would blue help you find and recognize the app quickly among your other apps?**

That's actually an interesting question because when I look at my sports and betting apps, several of them are blue. So while blue feels category-appropriate, it doesn't necessarily create strong differentiation. If you're competing against other blue betting apps, you'll need something beyond just the color to stand out—maybe a distinctive icon shape or strong brand mark.

From a pure recognition standpoint, blue is familiar but not distinctive. I'd probably recognize the app more by the "YES" branding than by the blue background.

---

**Q4: Does blue give you any specific associations or feelings (energy, fun, caution, cheap, premium, trust, etc.)?**

Blue is strongly associated with trust, credibility, and professionalism. In the betting industry, where regulatory compliance and financial security are critical, blue makes a lot of sense. It signals that the platform is legitimate, licensed, and trustworthy—which matters a great deal when you're putting real money on the line.

In terms of premium vs. cheap, it depends on the shade. A rich, deep blue can feel premium and sophisticated—think high-end financial services. A lighter or brighter blue might feel more accessible and casual, which isn't necessarily bad, just different positioning.

What blue doesn't convey as strongly is energy, excitement, or that adrenaline rush that comes with live betting. So if the brand strategy is to emphasize the thrill and excitement of sports betting, blue might not be doing that heavy lifting. But if the strategy is to emphasize trust, security, and professionalism, blue is a strong choice.

---

**Q5: On a scale of 1-5, how much do you like the idea of a blue app icon? (1=hate it, 5=love it)**

I'd give it a 4. Blue is a smart, strategic choice for a betting platform because trust is absolutely critical in this industry. You need users to feel confident that their money is safe, that the odds are fair, and that the platform is regulated. Blue communicates that effectively.

The reason it's not a 5 is that blue doesn't differentiate you strongly from competitors who also use blue, and it doesn't create that visceral excitement that can drive engagement during high-stakes moments. But overall, it's a solid choice.

---

**Q6: Any other thoughts about blue as our brand color for the app icon?**

If you go with blue, I'd recommend thinking carefully about the shade and how it's executed. A deep, saturated blue can feel premium and trustworthy. A washed-out or overly bright blue can feel generic. The design execution will be critical to making blue feel distinctive rather than just safe.

I'd also consider how the blue icon works in context—how does it look next to other betting apps? How does it stand out on a home screen filled with other blue icons? The branding and typography will need to do a lot of the differentiation work that the color alone won't accomplish.
