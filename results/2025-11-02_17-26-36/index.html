ğŸ”¬ Research Methodology

Test Design

This research used isolated context testing to ensure unbiased results. Each of the 100 test rounds was conducted independently with no memory or influence from previous tests.

Questions Asked to Each Persona

Q1: Preference

"Which tagline would you most likely click on?"

Measures initial appeal and acquisition potential. Tests what drives user engagement and curiosity.

Q2: Reasoning

"Why did you choose that tagline?"

Captures qualitative insights about what appeals to different personas and why certain messaging resonates.

Q3: Rejection

"Which tagline would you least likely click on?"

Identifies messaging that actively repels users. Critical for avoiding costly marketing mistakes.

Q4: Trust

"Which tagline suggests a trustworthy, premium gambling platform?"

Measures credibility and conversion potential. Tests what builds confidence for deposits and long-term engagement.

Test Parameters

ğŸ¯ 100 Isolated Tests

Each test round was completely independent, ensuring no context pollution or bias carryover between rounds.

ğŸ² Randomized Order

Taglines were presented in random order each round to prevent position bias affecting results.

ğŸ‘¥ 5 Personas

Diverse user archetypes covering casual to serious gamblers, ages 18-60+, different motivations.

ğŸŒ 2 Languages

English and Danish variants tested separately, recognizing cultural and linguistic differences in messaging.

âš ï¸ Why Isolated Context Matters

Traditional A/B testing can suffer from sequential bias where earlier exposures influence later responses. By running each test in complete isolation:

  • No learning effects: Personas don't "remember" previous taglines
  • No comparison bias: Each tagline evaluated on its own merits
  • No fatigue: Fresh perspective for every test
  • True preference: Results reflect actual first impressions
