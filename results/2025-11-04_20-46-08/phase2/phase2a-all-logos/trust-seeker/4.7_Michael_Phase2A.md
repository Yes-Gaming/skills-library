## Phase 2A Questions & Responses

### Q1: Overall Preference (Forced Choice)
**Question:** Which logo do you like the most overall? Why?

**Michael's Response:**
"Logo 2. Straightforward. Clear. No nonsense. I work in construction - when I see simple, clean work, I know the person knows what they're doing. When I see overcomplicated, fancy stuff, usually means they're covering up poor quality work. Same principle here. Logo 2 is clean, readable, professional. Gets the job done. That's what I want."

**Key Themes:**
- Tradesman's practical lens
- Simple = quality
- Fancy = covering up problems
- Functional approach
- Professional work standards

---

### Q2: Trust Leader (Forced Choice)
**Question:** Which one feels most trustworthy for a betting/gambling platform?

**Michael's Response:**
"Logo 2. Is it clear? Yes. Are the terms likely transparent? Looks like it. Simple and honest? That's what it suggests.

I've been an electrician for 25 years. You know what I've learned? The contractors who have simple, clear branding are the ones who do honest work and stand behind it. The ones with flashy logos and fancy designs often cut corners and hide problems.

Logo 3 and 6 with the script writing - that's like a contractor who spends more on their truck wrap than on quality tools. All show, no substance. Logo 4 is too aggressive - reminds me of pushy salespeople who won't give you a straight answer. Logo 1's tilted thing is gimmicky - unnecessary complications.

Logo 2 looks like a company that does straightforward business. Clear pricing, clear terms, no hidden fees. That's trustworthy. That's what I need."

**Trust Framework:**
- Professional parallels to construction
- Simple = honest work
- Flashy = hiding problems
- Straightforward = trustworthy
- Clear business practices expected

**Practical Skepticism:**
- Scripts = all show
- Aggressive = evasive
- Gimmicks = complications

---

### Q3: Standout/Visibility (Forced Choice)
**Question:** Which one would stand out most in an app store or advertisement?

**Michael's Response:**
"Logo 4 stands out most - it's loud. But standing out doesn't mean good. In my work, the loudest generator isn't always the most reliable. Often it's the opposite - noisy means problems.

Logo 2 would stand out to me in a different way. When I'm looking for tools or suppliers, I look for clear, professional presentation. That stands out more than flashy advertising. Same here - Logo 2 stands out by being straightforward and professional. That catches my attention because it suggests quality.

Practical question: which logo makes me think 'this company is reliable'? Logo 2. The others just make noise."

**Analysis:**
- Loud ≠ reliable (generator analogy)
- Professional = quality signal
- Practical evaluation over flash
- Reliability > attention

### Q4: Brand Fit (Forced Choice)
**Question:** Which one best represents "Play Made Easy"?

**Michael's Response:**
"Logo 2. Easy means simple and clear, not fancy. When a client asks me to 'make it easy,' they mean:
- Easy to understand the quote
- Easy to see what's included
- Easy to contact me if there's a problem
- Easy to trust it's done right

They don't mean 'make it casual' or 'make it flashy.' They mean straightforward and professional.

Logo 2 represents that kind of easy - clear terms, simple navigation, transparent pricing, no hidden complications. That's what 'easy' should mean for a gambling platform.

Logo 3 and 6 suggest 'easy' as casual and carefree - that's wrong for gambling. Money is serious. Logo 2 gets it right - simple and clear, but still professional."

**"Easy" Interpreted As:**
- Clear understanding
- Visible information
- Accessible support
- Trustworthy execution
- NOT casual or flashy

**Practical Translation:**
- Client relationship analogy
- Professional simplicity
- Serious about money

Logo 2 looks like a company that:

That's all I need. No hidden surprises, no complicated terms, no runaround. Just straightforward gambling entertainment with clear rules and honest dealing.

Logo 3, 6, 4, 1 - all look like companies that would make simple things complicated. I don't have time for that. I work long days, I want simple entertainment on weekends. Logo 2 looks like it would deliver that.

Straightforward wins. Would download Logo 2."

### Q7: Immediate Eliminations (Open)
**Question:** Which logos would you eliminate immediately? Why?

**Michael's Response:**
"Four eliminated immediately:

**Logo 3 and Logo 6 (Scripts) - Out**
Too fancy. Reminds me of companies that spend more on marketing than on service quality. In construction, when you see fancy branding, often means poor work quality underneath. These logos are all style, no substance. I need substance. Gambling involves my money - I need straightforward, not fancy. Eliminated.

**Logo 4 (Bold shadow) - Out**
Too aggressive and pushy. Like those salespeople at the hardware store who won't leave you alone. When someone's that aggressive, I don't trust them. Logo 4 tries too hard. Makes me think they're compensating for something - maybe poor service or unfair terms. I want straightforward dealing, not aggressive sales tactics. Eliminated.

**Logo 1 (Tilted stamp) - Out**
Gimmicky. Unnecessary complication. Why is it tilted? No practical reason. Just trying to be different. When I see unnecessary complications in branding, I worry about unnecessary complications in their service. Keep it simple and straight. Logo 1 doesn't do that. Eliminated.

Only Logo 2, 5, and 7 are straightforward and professional enough. The others fail basic practical test - are they clear and honest? No. So they're out."

**Practical Elimination Criteria:**
- Style over substance (scripts)
- Aggressive sales tactics (bold shadow)
- Unnecessary complications (tilted)
- Failed clarity test
- Failed honesty signal test
