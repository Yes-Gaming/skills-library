## Q1: Overall Preference (Forced Choice)
**Which logo do you like the most overall? Why?**

Logo 2. Well, I think the reason is that it demonstrates excellent typographic hierarchy and clean execution. The refined lowercase treatment shows attention to detail - the kerning is balanced, the proportions are professional, and it avoids the common pitfall of over-designing. From a game developer's perspective, this would scale beautifully across different screen sizes and resolutions, which is critical for modern casino games. The simplicity doesn't mean it's boring; it means the designer understood restraint and let the letterforms do the work. This is the kind of logo that would look sharp on a loading screen, app icon, or promotional material without losing clarity.

## Q2: Trust Leader (Forced Choice)
**Which one feels most trustworthy for a betting/gambling platform?**

## Q3: Standout/Visibility (Forced Choice)
**Which one would stand out most in an app store or advertisement?**

Logo 4. From a technical standpoint, the bold shadow treatment creates strong contrast and depth that would catch the eye in a crowded app store grid. However, I'd be concerned about how this scales - the shadow might become muddy at very small sizes or on lower-resolution displays.

## Q7: Immediate Eliminations (Open)
**Which logos would you eliminate immediately? Why?**

Logo 1 (Tilted stamp) - The tilted angle creates alignment challenges across UI elements and the distressed effect feels forced. From a technical perspective, that texture won't scale consistently across different rendering engines.

Logo 3 (Script) - The execution feels amateurish. The letterforms lack refinement and the script style is too loose for professional gaming branding. It would be difficult to maintain consistency across different applications.

Logo 7 (YS monogram) - Too minimal and lacks distinctiveness. Monograms work for established luxury brands but for a gaming platform, you need immediate name recognition. This would fail the app store visibility test because users wouldn't know what "YS" means without prior brand knowledge.

Logo 5 (Bold outline) - The outline treatment feels dated and the boldness crosses from confident into heavy-handed. The technical execution is fine, but the design direction feels like it's trying too hard to look "gaming" in a way that actually undermines sophistication.
