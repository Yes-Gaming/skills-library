## Q2: Trust Leader (Forced Choice)

The others all have some psychological angle - shadows for depth perception, rounded edges for comfort, distressed effects for authenticity. Logo 2 is just... straightforward typography. ## Q3: Standout/Visibility (Forced Choice)
**Which one would stand out most in an app store or advertisement?**

Logo 4 would definitely stand out, but that's exactly the problem. This seems designed to exploit attention-grabbing mechanisms. The shadow creates dimensional depth that our brains are wired to notice, and the boldness triggers arousal responses. It's effective, but it feels manipulative - like it's designed to bypass rational decision-making and trigger impulsive downloads.

## Q6: Top 3 Ranking (Ranked Choice)

## Q7: Immediate Eliminations (Open)
**Which logos would you eliminate immediately? Why?**

Logo 4 (Bold shadow) - This seems designed to trigger impulsive responses. The visual weight and contrast are calculated to grab attention and create excitement. From a psychology perspective, this is exactly the kind of branding that precedes predatory practices.

Logo 1 (Tilted stamp) - The distressed, vintage effect is trying to create false authenticity. It's a psychological trick to make something new seem established and trustworthy. I don't trust that.

Logo 3 (Script) - Trying too hard to look fun and friendly. This seems designed to make gambling feel casual and harmless, which is a dangerous psychological manipulation for an activity that can be addictive.
