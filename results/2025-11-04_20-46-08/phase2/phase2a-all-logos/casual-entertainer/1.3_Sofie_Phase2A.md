## Q7: IMMEDIATE ELIMINATIONS

**Question:** Which logos would you eliminate immediately? Why?

**Sofie's Response:**

**Logo 3 and Logo 6** - Immediate eliminations. These feel like textbook examples of predatory design masquerading as "friendly." I've studied this in my consumer psychology class - companies use soft, rounded, playful designs to lower your guard and make you feel safe so you'll spend more. The script/cursive style is deliberately chosen to feel personal and trustworthy, but it's a facade. These logos are designed to manipulate through false intimacy. Hard pass.

**Logo 5** - The thick black outline is just visual aggression. It's shouting "YES" at me, which feels like pressure to agree or comply. This is the kind of logo that would be on an app with constant push notifications and dark patterns to keep you engaged. The heaviness of the design mirrors heavy-handed marketing tactics.

**Logo 4** - The shadow effect is trying to create a sense of dimension and premium quality that probably isn't there. It's like when apps use glossy effects to seem more expensive than they are. This feels like compensation - if the app was actually good, why would the logo need visual tricks to seem impressive? The shadow is literally creating a false depth, which feels metaphorically appropriate for gambling apps that hide their odds.

I'd only consider Logo 2 and maybe Logo 7, though I'm skeptical of 7's exclusivity angle.
