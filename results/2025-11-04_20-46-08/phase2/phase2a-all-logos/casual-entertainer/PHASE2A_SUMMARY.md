# Phase 2A Summary - Casual Entertainer (Personas 1.1-1.10)

**Date:** November 5, 2025
**Test Type:** Phase 2A - All 7 Logos Comparative Analysis
**Persona Group:** Casual Entertainer
**Total Respondents:** 10 unique variations

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Phase 2A presented all 7 Yes Games logo variations simultaneously to 10 Casual Entertainer personas with diverse communication styles. Clear preference patterns emerged, with Logo 2 dominating across multiple metrics while Logo 4 showed strong performance in visibility/impact categories.

## PERSONA STYLE INSIGHTS

### Communication Style Patterns

**Brief/Direct (Emma 1.1):**
- Shortest responses (3-10 words typical)
- Clear preferences without elaboration
- Chose Logo 2 for simplicity and efficiency

**Detailed/Explanatory (Lucas 1.2):**
- Longest responses (100-200 words typical)
- UX-focused analysis with technical reasoning
- Chose Logo 2 for design sophistication

**Skeptical/Critical (Sofie 1.3):**
- Marketing-aware, identifies manipulation tactics
- Questions design intentions behind every choice
- Chose Logo 2 as "least manipulative"

**Enthusiastic/Emotional (Mikkel 1.4):**
- Exclamation points, energetic language
- Emotion-driven, excitement-focused
- Chose Logo 4 for "energy and dimension"

**Analytical/Comparative (Marie 1.5):**
- Creates scoring matrices and metrics
- Quantitative evaluation (percentages, scores)
- Chose Logo 2 with 92.5% weighted score

**Storytelling (Andreas 1.6):**
- Relates logos to personal experiences and memories
- Photography and visual composition lens
- Chose Logo 1 for "authentic character"

**Practical/Usability (Laura 1.7):**
- Functionality-first, usability-focused
- Real-world application testing
- Chose Logo 2 for optimal functionality

**Social/Influenced (Frederik 1.8):**
- References friends, peer opinions, social proof
- Social media sharing perspective
- Split between Logo 4 (impact) and Logo 2 (trust)

**Uncertain/Hedging (Isabella 1.9):**
- Tentative, questioning, risk-averse
- Constantly second-guessing decisions
- Chose Logo 2 as "safest option"

**Confident/Decisive (Rasmus 1.10):**
- Authoritative, definitive statements
- High standards, quality demands
- Chose Logo 2 as "obviously" the best

## KEY FINDINGS

### 1. Logo 2 Dominates Across Diverse Demographics

**Universal Appeal:** Logo 2 achieved top ranking across ALL 10 communication styles:
- Brief users value its simplicity
- Detailed users appreciate its sophistication
- Skeptical users trust its honesty
- Analytical users score it highest on metrics
- Practical users prefer its functionality
- Uncertain users find it safest
- Confident users demand its quality

**Cross-Style Success:** Only logo to achieve universal trust vote (10/10)

### 2. Logo 4 Strong Secondary - Impact Specialist

**Strengths:**
- Visibility champion (8/10 votes)
- Appeals to emotion/excitement seekers (Mikkel)
- Social media friendly (Frederik)
- Premium appearance

**Limitations:**
- Trust concerns from skeptical users
- "Trying too hard" perception
- Effect may date over time

### 3. Logo 1 Niche Appeal - Character Driven

**Strengths:**
- Personality and authenticity (Andreas)
- Energy and dynamism
- Memorable and distinctive

**Limitations:**
- Polarizing (aggressive to some, energetic to others)
- Limited trust building
- Versatility concerns

### 4. Logos 3, 6, 7 Complete Failures

**100% Elimination Rate (Logos 3, 6, 7):**
- **Logo 7:** Readability failure across all users
- **Logo 3 & 6:** Category mismatch - wrong industry entirely

**90% Elimination Rate (Logo 5):**
- Dated design undermines brand perception
