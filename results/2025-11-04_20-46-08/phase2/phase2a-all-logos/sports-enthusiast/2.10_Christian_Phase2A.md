### Q7: Immediate Eliminations (Open)
**Which logos would you eliminate immediately? Why?**

Logo 7 - eliminated immediately. Monograms are for established luxury brands with decades of equity, not emerging betting platforms. This shows fundamental misunderstanding of brand strategy. What serious bettor has time to decode "YS" when trying to place quick bets? It's pretentious without the credentials to back it up. Rejected.

Logos 3 and 6 - obviously eliminated. Script typography is completely inappropriate for sports betting. It's weak, overly decorative, and suggests the platform doesn't understand its own market. When I'm analyzing Premier League odds and placing strategic bets, I need sharp, efficient branding - not flourishes and curves. These logos would embarrass me if colleagues saw them on my phone. Unacceptable.

Logo 4 - eliminated despite its visibility. The bold shadow treatment is aggressive and unsophisticated. It's the visual equivalent of shouting, which is what low-quality platforms do to mask inadequate offerings. I've consulted for enough businesses to recognize when styling is compensating for lack of substance. Premium platforms don't need to be loud - they earn respect through quality. Logo 4 tries too hard and signals all the wrong things.

The decision is clear: Logo 2 is the only option that meets professional standards for a serious betting platform.
