### Q2: Trust Leader (Forced Choice)

### Q7: Immediate Eliminations (Open)
**Which logos would you eliminate immediately? Why?**

Logo 4 - immediately eliminated. That bold + shadow combination is straight out of the predatory betting app playbook. I've researched this: apps using aggressive visual styling statistically have worse responsible gambling features and higher complaint rates. The shadow effect is specifically designed to create urgency and trigger impulsive downloads.

Logos 3 and 6 - eliminated for different but equally problematic reasons. The script/cursive styling is a manipulation tactic to make gambling feel softer, gentler, more approachable. "Look how friendly and harmless we are!" This is dangerous because it downplays financial risk. I've interviewed problem gamblers who specifically mentioned how "cute" or "friendly" looking apps made them feel safer betting more than they should.

Logo 7 - eliminated because monograms in betting contexts are often used by operators trying to obscure their identity or create distance from regulatory scrutiny. Why not just say "Yes Games" clearly? What are you hiding behind "YS"? Maybe nothing, but as a journalist covering this industry, I've learned that lack of transparency in branding often correlates with lack of transparency in practices.

The betting industry has massive problems with predatory design, and logos are the first signal of whether a company is trustworthy or manipulative.
