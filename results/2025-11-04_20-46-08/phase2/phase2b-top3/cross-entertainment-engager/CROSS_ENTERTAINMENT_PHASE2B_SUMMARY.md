## Logo 1 & Logo 4 Consistent Weaknesses

### Logo 1 (Tilted Stamp) - Universally Ranked 3rd
**Key objections across all personas:**
- Tilt creates readability and compositional problems
- Too casino-specific, doesn't flex to sports/esports
- Feels promotional/temporary, not permanent platform brand
- Awkward in vertical content formats (Stories, Reels)
- Creates visual friction in unified platform navigation
- Stamp aesthetic limits cross-category adaptability

### Logo 4 (Shadow Effect) - Consistently Ranked 2nd
**Key objections across all personas:**
- Shadow effect feels dated (2015-era design)
- Rendering inconsistencies across devices and screen densities
- Sports-betting-heavy aesthetic, less versatile
- Shadow adds visual weight without functional value
- Doesn't adapt well to dark mode or varied backgrounds
- Creates micro-friction in context switching

## Critical Insights for Unified Platforms

### 1. Integration Over Category Signaling
Cross-Entertainment Engagers overwhelmingly prefer logos that signal **platform integration** over **category specificity**. They value brands that work across all entertainment types equally.

### 2. Versatility as Premium Signal
For multi-product users, **versatility itself signals quality**. A logo that adapts to sports, casino, esports without friction demonstrates the platform's integration competence.

### 3. Technical Consistency Builds Trust
**Rendering consistency** across devices, contexts, and interfaces creates unconscious trust signals. Visual fragmentation (even subtle) triggers concerns about operational integration.

### 4. Modern Premium = Refined Simplicity
Cross-Entertainment personas (especially higher-income, tech-savvy users) associate **premium with restraint**, not decoration. Effects and tilts signal trying to impress, not inherent excellence.

### 5. Content Sharing Amplifies Brand Value
For social and content-creator segments, logos must work in **screenshot sharing, streaming overlays, and social media contexts**. Clean, simple designs have massive advantage in social amplification.

## Persona-Specific Nuances

### Power Users (Kristian, Ida, Sebastian)
- Demand excellence and strategic brand architecture
- Value cross-device technical consistency
- Expect premium positioning to match spending level
- Think in terms of platform evolution and category expansion

### Content Creators (Alexander, Mads, Daniel)
- Need logo to work in content creation contexts
- Value social shareability and visual cleanliness
- Think about brand in streaming, videos, social posts
- Require versatility across different content formats

### Practical Efficiency Users (Marcus, Camilla)
- Focus on functional performance and time efficiency
- Value reduced cognitive load and visual friction
- Think in terms of quick context switching
- Need simplicity that supports fast decision-making

### Uncertain/Trust-Building Users (Julie, Nanna)
- Appreciate approachable, non-intimidating design
- Value signals of reliability and consistency
- Skeptical of platforms that overpromise
- Need friendly affirmation, not aggressive commands
