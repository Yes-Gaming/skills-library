Testing Journey

Three-phase methodology designed to eliminate bias and identify the strongest brand identity through sequential evaluation, comparative analysis, and finalist testing.

Phase 1: Sequential Individual Testing

Method: 50 personas tested 7 logos individually, one at a time in random order

Purpose: Establish baseline performance without comparative bias

Key Metrics: Trust, modernity, iGaming fit, memorability, market positioning

Outcome: Logo 2 achieved highest trust (4.1/5) and modernity (4.9/5) scores. Logo 4 led in category fit (4.1/5). Logo 1 was most memorable (58% recall).

Phase 2A: Full Comparative Testing

Method: 50 personas evaluated all 7 logos simultaneously

Purpose: Determine top 3 finalists through direct comparison

Key Finding: Logo 2 dominated with 80% preference (40/50 votes)

Outcome: Advanced Logos 2, 4, and 1 to Phase 2B. Eliminated Logos 3, 5, 6, 7 due to poor performance (100% elimination for script logos 3 & 6).

Phase 2B: Top 3 Finalist Testing

Method: 50 personas chose between top 3 (Logos 1, 2, 4)

Purpose: Final head-to-head to determine winner

Key Finding: Logo 2 strengthened to 72% preference (36/50 votes)

Outcome: Clear winner identified with decisive margin. Logo 2 performance INCREASED from 42% to 72% under scrutiny, demonstrating strength in final comparison.

The Trust Seeker Anomaly

Understanding the 40+ Preference for Logo 4

The Trust Seeker demographic (ages 44-56) is the ONLY demographic showing strong preference for Logo 4 over Logo 2 (90% vs 10%). This represents a significant strategic consideration that requires understanding and mitigation.

Why This Demographic Differs

  • Shadow Effect = Establishment Signal: Trust Seekers interpret Logo 4's drop shadow as "dimensional credibility," "solid establishment feeling," and "adds legitimacy and substance." The shadow effect is read as physical weight = trustworthiness.
  • Minimal Design = Insufficient Substance: Logo 2's clean, minimal aesthetic triggered concerns like "too light/insubstantial for financial transactions" and "lacks the gravitas expected from gambling operator." For Trust Seekers, visual simplicity = insufficient credibility signals.
  • Life Experience Creating Different Visual Preferences: This demographic's formative design experiences occurred in 1980s-2000s when legitimate businesses used dimensional logos (shadows, gradients, 3D effects). Visual literacy shaped by different era = different trust cues.
  • Risk Aversion in Financial Contexts: Trust Seekers handle larger average deposits, have more regulatory awareness, and greater loss aversion. They need stronger visual reassurance before trusting money to platform.
  • Skepticism Toward "Tech Startup" Aesthetics: Clean, minimal design associated with Silicon Valley tech startups (perceived as risky, unregulated) and cryptocurrencies (associated with scams). Logo 2's modern minimalism triggers "startup risk" mental model.

Mitigation Strategy for Logo 2

Since Logo 2 won overall but Trust Seekers prefer Logo 4, implement these compensatory trust-building measures to address the 40+ demographic's need for establishment signals:

  • Prominent Licensing Displays: Spillemyndigheden (Danish Gambling Authority) license badge in footer, license number on homepage, regulatory compliance statements, third-party certification badges
  • Security Certifications: SSL certification badges on registration/deposit pages, payment security icons (Visa Secure, Mastercard SecureCode), data protection certifications, bank-grade encryption messaging
  • Responsible Gambling Tools Prominence: Deposit limits tools on homepage, self-exclusion options clearly accessible, partnership with Spillebrikken (Danish helpline), transparent RTP information
  • Customer Testimonials and Social Proof: Trustpilot rating widget (aim for 4+ stars), age-diverse customer testimonials, "years of operation" statistics, industry awards/recognition badges
  • Professional Operations Signals: "About Us" page with company history, team photos, office locations, Danish phone number and physical address, press mentions, partnership logos
  • Traditional Design Elements in Marketing: Conservative color palette in Trust Seeker-targeted campaigns, dimensional/photographic imagery in ads, traditional typography in legal pages, professional photography

Expected Impact: Best of both worlds - modern brand appearance for 80% of demographics who prefer Logo 2, while explicit institutional signals serve the 20% who need traditional trust cues.
