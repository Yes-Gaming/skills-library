# User Research Interview: App Icon Design Concept
## Yes Games - RED Background Icon Testing

**Persona:** Nikolaj (Variation 2.5 - Analytical/Comparative Style)
**Age:** 34
**Location:** Copenhagen, Østerbro
**Date:** November 6, 2025
**Concept Tested:** RED app icon with YES logotype

---

## Interview Questions & Responses

**Q1: What's your first reaction when you see a RED app icon on your home screen?**

Comparing it to the competitive landscape, red is an outlier. Most established betting platforms use blues, greens, or blacks. Red positions you outside the traditional trust framework that those colors establish.

---

**Q2: How does RED make you feel in the context of a gaming/entertainment app?**

From a statistical betting perspective, red creates emotional arousal, which is counterproductive to rational decision-making. My betting is based on mathematical models and odds analysis. Red signals emotional betting, not strategic betting. That's a market positioning problem.

---

**Q3: Would RED help you find and recognize the app quickly among your other apps?**

Yes, recognition speed would be optimal. However, if we look at the data on premium brand perception, red underperforms compared to navy, black, or dark green. The trade-off is: fast recognition but lower perceived quality and trust.

---

**Q4: Does RED give you any specific associations or feelings (energy, fun, caution, cheap, premium, etc.)?**

Analyzing color psychology data: red scores high on urgency (78%), energy (81%), but low on trust (18%), premium perception (22%), and reliability (15%). Compare that to navy blue: trust (71%), premium (58%), reliability (69%). The metrics don't favor red for high-value users like me who prioritize platform credibility.

---

**Q5: On a scale of 1-5, how much do you like the idea of a RED app icon? (1=hate it, 5=love it)**

2. The analytical case against red is strong. It optimizes for emotional engagement at the expense of trust and premium positioning. For someone running a data-driven betting system with significant monthly volume, red signals the wrong platform values. I'd migrate to a competitor with more sophisticated branding.

---

**Q6: Any other thoughts about RED as our brand color for the app icon?**

Looking at platform evaluation metrics: market depth, odds accuracy, data reliability, and brand trust all matter to analytical bettors. Red undermines the trust component without adding value to the other metrics. A dark, sophisticated color palette would better align with the high-value bettor segment. Red optimizes for a different user profile—one I don't fit.
