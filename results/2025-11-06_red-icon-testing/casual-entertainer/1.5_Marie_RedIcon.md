# User Research Interview: App Icon Design Concept
## Yes Games - RED Background Icon Testing

**Persona:** Marie (Variation 1.5 - Analytical/Comparative Style)
**Age:** 26
**Location:** Copenhagen, Østerbro
**Date:** November 6, 2025
**Concept Tested:** RED app icon with YES logotype

---

## Interview Questions & Responses

**Q1: What's your first reaction when you see a RED app icon on your home screen?**

Comparing it to standard app design conventions, red is unusual. Most successful apps use blues, whites, or brand-specific colors. Red is typically reserved for notifications or error states, so using it as a primary brand color inverts standard UX patterns.

---

**Q2: How does RED make you feel in the context of a gaming/entertainment app?**

If we look at the data on color psychology, red increases arousal and urgency. In gambling contexts, that could be interpreted as strategic—keeping users engaged—but it could also backfire by creating stress. Compared to competitors who use calmer colors, this is a high-risk, high-energy approach.

---

**Q3: Would RED help you find and recognize the app quickly among your other apps?**

Statistically, yes. Red has the highest attention-capture rate of any color. Recognition speed would likely be 30-50% faster than neutral colors. However, recognition efficiency isn't the only metric—brand sentiment matters equally.

---

**Q4: Does RED give you any specific associations or feelings (energy, fun, caution, cheap, premium, etc.)?**

Looking at the data: Red is associated with urgency (72% of respondents in color studies), danger/caution (64%), energy (81%), but premium quality only (23%). Compare that to navy blue, which scores 68% on trust and 54% on premium. Gold scores 76% on premium. Red is statistically disadvantaged for quality perception.

---

**Q5: On a scale of 1-5, how much do you like the idea of a RED app icon? (1=hate it, 5=love it)**

3. The analytical case for red is mixed. High visibility, high energy, but low trust and low premium perception. If the goal is user acquisition through distinctiveness, it scores well. If the goal is retention through trust, it scores poorly. The strategic alignment determines the rating.

---

**Q6: Any other thoughts about RED as our brand color for the app icon?**

From a comparative analysis standpoint: Netflix uses red successfully, but they're entertainment with no financial risk. Banks never use red—they use blues and greens for trust. Gambling is a hybrid: entertainment with financial risk. Red optimizes for one dimension (excitement) while sacrificing another (trust). A dual-tone approach (red accents on dark base) might balance both objectives better.
