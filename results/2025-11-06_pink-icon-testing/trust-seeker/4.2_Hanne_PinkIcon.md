# User Research Interview: App Icon Design Concept
## Yes Games - Pink Background Icon Testing

**Persona:** Hanne (Variation 4.2 - Detailed/Explanatory Style)
**Age:** 52
**Location:** Copenhagen, Gentofte
**Date:** November 6, 2025
**Concept Tested:** Pink app icon with YES logotype

---

## Interview Questions & Responses

**Q1: What's your first reaction when you see a pink app icon on your iPhone home screen?**

My first reaction is confusion, honestly. Pink doesn't match my mental model of what a legitimate, licensed gambling platform should look like. When I think about trustworthy gambling operators—ones that are properly regulated by Spillemyndigheden, have clear responsible gambling tools, and maintain transparent terms—I don't picture pink.

Pink reads as youthful, playful, perhaps even frivolous. But gambling is a financial activity with real risks and real consequences. I need to feel confident that the platform takes that seriously. Pink doesn't communicate seriousness to me.

---

**Q2: How does pink make you feel in the context of a gaming/entertainment app?**

Pink makes me feel uncertain, which is not what I want from a gambling platform. Let me explain why:

As someone who's been gambling responsibly for years, I've learned to evaluate platforms based on specific trust signals:
1. **Visible licensing information** (Danish and EU)
2. **Clear responsible gambling tools** (deposit limits, self-exclusion)
3. **Transparent terms and conditions** (no hidden clauses)
4. **Professional customer support** (responsive, helpful)
5. **Secure payment processing** (recognized payment providers)

None of those things are communicated by the color of an app icon, but the icon does set expectations. Pink sets an expectation of casualness and fun, not seriousness and security. That creates a disconnect for me.

If I see a pink gambling app, my immediate questions are:
- "Is this a licensed operator or some fly-by-night startup?"
- "Do they have proper security measures in place?"
- "Will they handle withdrawals professionally?"
- "Are they compliant with Danish gambling regulations?"

Pink doesn't answer any of those questions positively. It raises them as concerns.

---

**Q3: Would pink help you find and recognize the app quickly among your other apps?**

Yes, pink would be easy to spot. But that's solving a problem I don't really have. I don't struggle to find my gambling apps—I have maybe two or three, and I know exactly where they are.

What I do struggle with is knowing which platforms I can trust. Visibility isn't my concern; trustworthiness is. And pink doesn't help with that. If anything, it creates doubt.

---

**Q4: Does pink give you any specific associations or feelings (energy, fun, caution, cheap, premium, trust, etc.)?**

Pink gives me several associations, and most of them don't align with what I need from a gambling platform:

**What pink signals to me:**
- **Playfulness and lightness** (strong association)
- **Youthfulness** (it feels aimed at a younger demographic)
- **Trendiness** (following current aesthetic trends)
- **Approachability** (trying to seem friendly and non-threatening)

**What pink does NOT signal:**
- **Trust and security** (critical for gambling)
- **Professionalism and legitimacy** (I need to feel it's a serious operation)
- **Regulatory compliance** (pink doesn't say "licensed and regulated")
- **Financial stability** (pink doesn't inspire confidence in their business)

When I compare pink to other colors I associate with trustworthy gambling platforms:
- **Dark blue:** Trust, security, professionalism (exactly what I want)
- **Dark green:** Money, stability, traditional (reassuring)
- **Gray or black:** Serious, professional, established (trustworthy)
- **Pink:** Fun, young, trendy (not reassuring for gambling)

The gap is significant. Pink optimizes for visibility and approachability, but I don't need a gambling app to be approachable—I need it to be trustworthy.

---

**Q5: On a scale of 1-5, how much do you like the idea of a pink app icon? (1=hate it, 5=love it)**

**2 out of 5.**

I'm giving it a 2 because I don't hate pink as a concept, and I can see that it would be distinctive. But it fails on the most important criterion for me: trust.

Here's my reasoning:

**Pros (+1 point):**
- High visibility and easy recognition
- Shows willingness to be different and take creative risks
- Could appeal to younger users who value approachability

**Cons (-3 points):**
- **Does not signal trust or security** (deal-breaker for me)
- Feels unprofessional for a financial service (gambling involves real money)
- Raises questions about whether the operator is serious and legitimate
- Doesn't align with traditional markers of regulatory compliance

If I were evaluating two gambling platforms—one with a professional dark blue icon and clear licensing information, and one with a pink icon—I'd choose the blue one every time, assuming all else is equal. The blue one feels safer, more established, more trustworthy.

Now, if the pink platform could demonstrate exceptional trustworthiness in other ways—prominent licensing badges, industry-leading responsible gambling tools, transparent RTP information, excellent customer support—I might overcome my initial hesitation. But the icon alone makes me less likely to try it in the first place.

---

**Q6: Any other thoughts about pink as our brand color for the app icon?**

A few thoughts from the perspective of someone who's gambled responsibly for many years:

**1. Trust takes time to build, seconds to lose.**
In gambling, trust is everything. Users are handing you their money and personal information. If your branding raises any doubt—even subconsciously—about your legitimacy or professionalism, you'll lose users before they even open the app.

Pink raises doubt for me. It makes me wonder: "Is this a serious, licensed operator? Or is this some trendy startup that might not be around in a year?"

**2. Consider your target demographic.**
If Yes Games is targeting my demographic—people in their 40s, 50s, and beyond who have disposable income and gamble regularly—pink is a poor choice. We grew up with different cultural associations for pink, and we tend to value traditional markers of trust and professionalism.

If Yes Games is targeting people in their 20s and 30s who are more open to non-traditional branding and value approachability over formality, pink might work better. But know that you're making a choice: appeal to younger users at the cost of alienating older, potentially higher-value users.

**3. Pink doesn't fix underlying trust issues.**
If Yes Games has excellent responsible gambling tools, clear licensing, transparent terms, and professional support, then I might use it despite the pink icon. But if Yes Games is just average on those fronts, pink will actively hurt you.

Color can't compensate for poor operational practices. But it can amplify good ones or undermine them. Pink, for me, undermines trust unless it's backed up by exceptional substance.

**4. Test with real users in my demographic.**
I'd strongly encourage Yes Games to test pink specifically with users over 40 who gamble regularly. Ask them:
- Does pink make you more or less likely to trust this platform?
- Does pink signal security and legitimacy, or does it raise concerns?
- Would you choose a pink gambling app over a blue one, all else being equal?

My prediction: most users in my demographic will have concerns similar to mine. Pink will score well on visibility but poorly on trust. And for gambling, trust matters more than visibility.

**Final recommendation:**
If Yes Games wants to build trust with users like me—people who gamble regularly, have money to spend, and value security and professionalism—pink is the wrong choice. Choose a color that signals trust, stability, and legitimacy. Blue, gray, dark green, even a sophisticated purple would all work better.

But if Yes Games is explicitly targeting younger, more casual users and is willing to sacrifice trust signals for approachability, pink could be defensible. Just know what you're giving up.
