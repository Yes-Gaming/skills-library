# User Research Interview: App Icon Design Concept
## Yes Games - Pink Background Icon Testing

**Persona:** Lucas (Variation 1.2 - Detailed/Explanatory Style)
**Age:** 27
**Location:** Aarhus, Midtbyen
**Date:** November 6, 2025
**Concept Tested:** Pink app icon with YES logotype

---

## Interview Questions & Responses

**Q1: What's your first reaction when you see a pink app icon on your iPhone home screen?**

Well, my first thought is that it's unexpected—in a good way, actually. Pink is such an underutilized color in the gaming and gambling space that it immediately creates differentiation. From a design perspective, it's brave. Most competitors go for dark blues, reds, or blacks to signal trust or excitement. Pink flips that entirely.

That said, I'd want to see the execution. The shade of pink matters immensely. A hot pink feels youthful and trendy, like a social app. A dusty rose or salmon pink could feel more premium and approachable. The typography pairing would be crucial too—if you put "YES" in a bold sans-serif on pink, it either looks incredibly modern or accidentally juvenile, depending on weight and spacing.

---

**Q2: How does pink make you feel in the context of a gaming/entertainment app?**

Pink gives me associations of accessibility and friendliness, which I think could work really well for casual gaming. It doesn't feel intimidating or hyper-aggressive like some gambling apps that use dark reds or golds to signal high stakes.

Pink has this interesting psychological effect—it's inviting without being pushy. It's not screaming "WIN BIG NOW" like a slot machine. Instead, it whispers "come have fun." That could be disarming in a positive way, especially for users who might feel gambling is too serious or risky. Pink makes it feel lighter, more social, less "casino floor" and more "game night with friends."

But I'd be curious about gender perceptions. Pink has historically been coded feminine in Western culture, though that's changing with brands like T-Mobile and Barbie reclaiming it as bold and confident. For a gambling app, you'd need to ensure it doesn't alienate male users who might unconsciously associate pink with "not for me."

---

**Q3: Would pink help you find and recognize the app quickly among your other apps?**

Absolutely. Pink would create immediate visual hierarchy on my home screen. I don't think I have a single pink app icon right now—most are blue, green, red, or multicolor. Pink would be the only one in that color family, so recognition speed would be extremely fast.

From a UX standpoint, that's valuable. If I'm pulling out my phone quickly on a Sunday afternoon to check a bet or play a quick slot game, I want zero friction. A pink icon would be like a beacon—no hunting, no confusion. It's distinctive.

The risk, though, is that distinctiveness could work against you if users feel the color choice is "off-brand" for what they expect from a gambling platform. If someone mentally categorizes Yes Games as "the pink one," that's great for recall. But if they also think "the pink one is the less serious one," that could undermine trust or competitiveness perceptions.

---

**Q4: Does pink give you any specific associations or feelings (energy, fun, caution, cheap, premium, trust, etc.)?**

Pink gives me:
- **Fun and playfulness** (strong association)
- **Approachability and friendliness** (very strong)
- **Optimism and lightness** (moderate)
- **Modernity and trendiness** (if executed well)

It does NOT give me:
- **Trust or security** (that's more blue or gray territory)
- **Premium or luxury** (unless it's a very specific dusty rose with gold accents)
- **High stakes or intensity** (that's red or black)

So the strategic question is: what do you want your brand to communicate? If Yes Games wants to be the friendly, accessible, modern gambling platform that removes the intimidation factor, pink could be genius. It signals "we're different, we're approachable, we're not your grandfather's betting site."

But if you need to communicate trust, security, and high-stakes professionalism—especially to older demographics or serious sports bettors—pink might undermine that. It's a trade-off.

---

**Q5: On a scale of 1-5, how much do you like the idea of a pink app icon? (1=hate it, 5=love it)**

I'd give it a **4 out of 5**.

Here's why: As a designer, I love bold choices that break category conventions. Pink is unexpected, memorable, and creates instant differentiation. It shows confidence—you're not following the pack. That appeals to me.

I'm docking one point because I'd need to see the full execution before committing to a 5. The shade of pink, the typography, the YES logotype design, the consistency across the app experience—all of that would determine whether this is "brilliantly disruptive" or "accidentally off-brand."

But conceptually? I think it's a smart move. It positions Yes Games as modern, friendly, and confident. That's a strong brand foundation if you can deliver on it inside the app.

---

**Q6: Any other thoughts about pink as our brand color for the app icon?**

A few strategic considerations:

First, **test it with your actual target demographics.** Pink might resonate differently with 24-year-old casual gamers (like me) versus 45-year-old sports bettors or 50-year-old casino players. If your core audience is older and more conservative, pink could feel too playful. If you're targeting Gen Z and younger Millennials, pink could be perfect.

Second, **own it fully.** If you go pink, don't apologize for it or dilute it. Pink only works if you commit to it as a brand statement. Half-hearted pink—like a pale, washed-out version paired with generic design—would feel worse than just going with a safe blue.

Third, **consider the competitive landscape.** If no one else in the Danish gambling market is using pink, that's either a blue ocean opportunity or a signal that pink doesn't work for this category. Do the research to understand why competitors avoid it. Is it because they're all copying each other, or because users genuinely don't respond to pink for gambling?

Finally, **pair it with trust signals.** If you go pink for approachability, you'll need to compensate with strong trust cues inside the app—licensing badges, responsible gambling tools, clear terms, professional support. Pink can open the door, but trust has to close the deal.

Overall, I think it's a strong concept with high risk and high reward potential. Execute it well, and Yes Games could own a unique position in the market. Execute it poorly, and it'll feel like a gimmick.
