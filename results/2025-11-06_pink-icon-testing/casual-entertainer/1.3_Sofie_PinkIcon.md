# User Research Interview: App Icon Design Concept
## Yes Games - Pink Background Icon Testing

**Persona:** Sofie (Variation 1.3 - Skeptical/Critical Style)
**Age:** 23
**Location:** Copenhagen, Vesterbro
**Date:** November 6, 2025
**Concept Tested:** Pink app icon with YES logotype

---

## Interview Questions & Responses

**Q1: What's your first reaction when you see a pink app icon on your iPhone home screen?**

My first thought is: "Who is this for?" Because pink feels like a very calculated choice designed to stand out by being different, but I'm not sure it's different in a meaningful way. It feels like a marketing team said, "Let's do something nobody else is doing," without asking if it actually makes sense.

---

**Q2: How does pink make you feel in the context of a gaming/entertainment app?**

Skeptical, honestly. Pink in gambling feels like it's trying to soften something that shouldn't be softened. Gambling is inherently risky. It involves real money, addiction risks, and serious stakes. Pink feels like it's trying to make that feel cute or friendly, and that bothers me.

It's like putting a flower sticker on a cigarette pack. You're still selling something that can be harmful, but now you're wrapping it in a friendly color to make people feel less cautious. That feels manipulative to me. If Yes Games is trying to signal "we're safe and fun," I'd rather see that backed up with responsible gambling tools and transparency, not just a color choice.

---

**Q3: Would pink help you find and recognize the app quickly among your other apps?**

Sure, it'd stand out. But standing out isn't the same as being memorable for the right reasons. I'd find it, but I might also wonder if I downloaded the wrong app. Pink doesn't signal "gambling" to me. It signals beauty, wellness, maybe social media. Not betting on football or playing slots.

---

**Q4: Does pink give you any specific associations or feelings (energy, fun, caution, cheap, premium, trust, etc.)?**

Pink gives me:
- **Cuteness** (which feels wrong for gambling)
- **Friendliness** (which could be masking something)
- **Femininity** (which might alienate some users)
- **Trendiness** (which feels shallow)

It does NOT give me trust. It doesn't make me feel like this is a serious, licensed, secure platform. Pink feels like aesthetic branding, not substance. And in gambling, I care way more about substance—like clear licensing, fair odds, and responsible gambling features—than I do about looking trendy.

---

**Q5: On a scale of 1-5, how much do you like the idea of a pink app icon? (1=hate it, 5=love it)**

**2 out of 5.**

I don't hate it enough to give it a 1, because I can see the strategic logic—differentiation, approachability, breaking conventions. But I don't like it because it feels like style over substance. Pink doesn't solve any real user problem. It doesn't make me trust Yes Games more. It doesn't make gambling safer or more transparent. It just makes the app look different.

If Yes Games backs up the pink icon with genuinely friendly, transparent, responsible practices inside the app, maybe I'd bump it to a 3. But if it's just pink wrapping on the same predatory mechanics every other gambling app uses, then it's worse than just using blue. At least blue doesn't pretend to be your friend.

---

**Q6: Any other thoughts about pink as our brand color for the app icon?**

Yeah—ask yourselves why you're choosing pink. Is it because it genuinely represents your brand values and user experience? Or is it because you want to stand out in the App Store and think pink will get more downloads?

If it's the former, great. Build a gambling platform that actually lives up to the friendly, approachable promise of pink. Make your responsible gambling tools the best in the industry. Be radically transparent about odds and licensing. Show users you care about them as people, not just revenue sources.

If it's the latter—if pink is just a gimmick—then I'd advise against it. Users aren't stupid. We can tell when a brand is performing friendliness versus actually being friendly. And if Yes Games uses pink as aesthetic differentiation while operating like every other exploitative gambling app, that's going to backfire. People will feel tricked.

So my advice: Only go pink if you're willing to back it up with substance. Otherwise, it's just lipstick on a slot machine.
