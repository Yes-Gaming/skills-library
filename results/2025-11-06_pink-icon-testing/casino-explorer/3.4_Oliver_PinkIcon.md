# User Research Interview: App Icon Design Concept
## Yes Games - Pink Background Icon Testing

**Persona:** Oliver (Variation 3.4 - Enthusiastic/Emotional Style)
**Age:** 22
**Location:** Odense, city center
**Date:** November 6, 2025
**Concept Tested:** Pink app icon with YES logotype

---

## Interview Questions & Responses

**Q1: What's your first reaction when you see a pink app icon on your iPhone home screen?**

Whoa, that's BOLD! Pink for a gaming app? That's... unexpected! But I kinda dig it? It's different, man. Everything else is so dark and serious. Pink is like, "We're here to have FUN!"

---

**Q2: How does pink make you feel in the context of a gaming/entertainment app?**

Pink feels FRESH! It's playful, it's got personality. Some casino apps are so try-hard with their gold and black and "oooh we're so fancy" vibes. Pink is like, "Nah, we're just here for a good time." I respect that!

It's also super modern. Like, pink is having a moment right now. Streetwear brands, esports teams, content creators—everyone's using pink. It feels current, you know? Not stuck in the past like some old-school Vegas aesthetic.

---

**Q3: Would pink help you find and recognize the app quickly among your other apps?**

Dude, YES! Pink would POP on my screen! I've got like 100 apps and they all blend together. Pink would be the one I'd see instantly. Super convenient when I want to jump in and play something.

---

**Q4: Does pink give you any specific associations or feelings (energy, fun, caution, cheap, premium, trust, etc.)?**

Pink gives me:
- **FUN energy!** (not crazy intense, but good vibes)
- **Playful and chill** (like, it doesn't take itself too seriously)
- **Modern and trendy** (feels very 2025)
- **Confident** (takes guts to be pink when everyone else is dark)

It doesn't give me:
- **Hardcore gamer vibes** (pink is more chill than intense)
- **Expensive/luxury** (but that's okay, accessible is cool too)
- **Super serious** (which is fine, gambling should be fun anyway!)

Honestly, pink feels like a brand that knows who they are and isn't trying to be something they're not. That's fire!

---

**Q5: On a scale of 1-5, how much do you like the idea of a pink app icon? (1=hate it, 5=love it)**

**4 out of 5!**

I really like it! It's bold, it's different, it's got personality. Most gaming apps look the same, so pink stands out in the best way. It feels confident and modern.

I'm not giving it a full 5 because I'd want to see the actual execution. Like, what shade of pink? How's the logo styled? Is it sleek or is it kinda basic? The concept is great, but execution matters a ton.

If they nail the design—like, a really nice shade of pink with crisp typography and maybe some subtle gradients or textures—it could be SICK. But if it's just flat hot pink with basic text, it might look cheap.

---

**Q6: Any other thoughts about pink as our brand color for the app icon?**

DO IT! Seriously, the gaming and gambling space needs more personality. Everyone's copying each other with dark themes and serious vibes. Pink breaks the mold.

Pink also has this cool thing where it works for everyone. It's not just "for girls" anymore—look at Tyler the Creator, A$AP Rocky, all the streetwear collabs. Pink is for anyone who's confident enough to wear it. Same energy for an app icon.

My only advice: commit to it! Don't do pink half-heartedly. If you go pink, OWN it. Make the app inside match the vibe—fun, modern, engaging, high-quality. Pink sets an expectation. Deliver on it, and you'll have a super memorable brand.

Also, the content potential is INSANE. Pink app + cool UI + fun games = super shareable on TikTok, Instagram, YouTube. That's free marketing right there. Pink is content-worthy.

Overall, I'm hyped about this! It's a bold move, and I love brands that take bold moves. Just make sure the app lives up to the hype. Pink is the opening act—make sure the show is worth it!
