# Rasmus Knudsen - Cross-Entertainment UX Color Testing
**Profile:** 25-year-old gamer from Aalborg, Denmark | eSports + Traditional Betting Combo User | High Gaming-Quality UX Standards

## VARIATION 1: Deep Blue & Gold

### Q1: First Impression (1-5)
**Rating: 4/5** - Solid. Reminds me of premium gaming clients like Steam's dark mode meets Discord. The blue-gold combo feels sophisticated but not flashy enough for modern betting platforms. Gives "classic" vibes rather than "cutting edge."

### Q2: Color Harmony & Contrast
**Rating: 4/5** - Excellent harmony. The gold pops nicely against the deep blue background. High contrast = readable. No eye strain during extended sessions—critical for my use case. However, the blue feels slightly muted compared to what Epic Games or Twitch use.

### Q3: Brand Recognition
**Rating: 3/5** - Feels generic. Too many platforms use similar palettes. Doesn't scream "eSports betting platform" to me. I'd want something more distinctive that signals speed, energy, and real-time action.

### Q4: Text Readability
**Rating: 5/5** - Perfect. Gold text on deep blue is crisp. No squinting required on any font size I can imagine. This is how it should be done.

### Q5: Call-to-Action Buttons
**Rating: 3/5** - The gold works as accent but feels subdued for CTAs. In gaming, buttons need to feel responsive and pressable. This looks a bit too formal. Would prefer more vibrant highlight on hover states.

### Q6: Modern Aesthetic Score
**Rating: 3/5** - Feels slightly dated. The gold-on-blue combo has been done to death since 2015. Modern platforms (like current FaZe Clan website or new CS2 updates) use more aggressive color strategies. Feels safe but not innovative.

### Q7: Mobile Responsiveness Feeling
**Rating: 4/5** - Looks like it would adapt well to smaller screens. The contrast remains strong even if I squint. This is important for betting on the go—I'm often placing bets between matches or during streams.

### Q8: Engagement Factor
**Rating: 3/5** - Calming but not exciting. For a betting platform where I'm making real-time decisions on eSports odds, this feels too sedate. I want color to communicate urgency and opportunity.

### Q9: Accessibility (Colorblind-Friendly)
**Rating: 3/5** - Moderate risk. Blue-gold might be problematic for red-green colorblind users. Should test with accessibility validators. Blue-yellow spectrum is generally safer though.

### Q10: Betting-Specific Usability
**Rating: 4/5** - Good for displaying odds and information hierarchies. The contrast helps me quickly scan live scores and line changes. No distractions from key data points.

### Q11: eSports Integration Feel
**Rating: 3/5** - Doesn't match the kinetic energy of eSports environments. Twitch chat is chaotic orange-purple energy; Discord is sleek dark mode. This sits awkwardly in between—premium but disconnected from the gaming culture vibe.

### Q12: Overall Preference
**Rating: 3.5/5** - Professional, readable, solid. But for a 25-year-old gamer expecting Steam-level sophistication combined with Twitch energy? This feels like my dad's banking app got redesigned. Functional but uninspired.
