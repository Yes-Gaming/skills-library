## VARIANT 1 - Blue/Purple Base

### Q1: First Impression (0-10)
**Score: 7/10**

As someone who analyzes spreadsheets all day, this color scheme feels familiar and professional. The blue tones remind me of financial dashboards I use at work. It's clean, but perhaps a bit too corporate for a betting app where I want to feel some excitement during game time.

### Q2: Professionalism (0-10)
**Score: 9/10**

Excellent professional appearance. The blue-purple gradient conveys trust and stability - critical when real money is involved. This looks like an app that takes security seriously, which matters when I'm linking my bank account.

### Q3: Odds Readability (0-10)
**Score: 8/10**

The decimal odds are clearly visible against the darker backgrounds. The contrast is good for quick scanning. However, I'd like to see more differentiation between favorite and underdog odds at a glance. Currently, I have to read the numbers rather than pattern-match.

### Q4: Live Match Information Clarity (0-10)
**Score: 6/10**

The "LIVE" indicator is visible but doesn't create urgency. When I'm tracking multiple games simultaneously, I need instant visual feedback about which matches are active. The score display is adequate but could be more prominent for quick updates while watching TV.

### Q5: Call-to-Action Button Effectiveness (0-10)
**Score: 7/10**

The "Place bet" button is identifiable, but it doesn't trigger an immediate action response. When I've done my analysis and am ready to commit funds, I want that button to feel confident and decisive - this feels a bit passive.

### Q6: Emotional Response
**Rating: Neutral/Slightly Positive**

This color scheme makes me feel like I'm at work, which is both good and bad. Good because it reinforces the analytical, data-driven approach I take to betting. Bad because after analyzing for 30 minutes, I want some emotional payoff when placing a bet. It's too clinical.

### Q7: Trust Level (0-10)
**Score: 9/10**

Very high trust factor. The professional color palette suggests a legitimate, regulated platform. As someone managing substantial betting funds ($500-2000 per week), I need to know my money is safe. This achieves that completely.

### Q8: Ease of Finding Key Information (0-10)
**Score: 7/10**

Information hierarchy is logical, but everything blends together at a similar visual weight. When I'm comparing 15-20 different betting options, I need clear visual separation between teams, odds, and supplementary stats. Currently requires too much cognitive processing.

### Q9: Visual Fatigue (0-10, lower = more fatigue)
**Score: 6/10**

After 15 minutes of analysis, the blue starts to feel monotonous. I often spend 45-60 minutes before a game day analyzing matchups, and this color scheme doesn't maintain engagement over that timeframe. Needs more visual variety to sustain focus.

### Q10: Suitability for Extended Use (0-10)
**Score: 6/10**

Decent for short sessions, but during a full Sunday of NFL or Saturday college football where I'm in the app 6-8 hours, this feels flat. I need visual elements that maintain mental alertness without causing strain.

### Q11: Preference vs. Current Apps (0-10)
**Score: 6/10**

Compared to DraftKings and FanDuel, this is more subdued. While I appreciate the professionalism, those apps do better at balancing excitement with functionality. For a statistical bettor, I need cold efficiency, but I'm still human and want some emotional engagement.

### Q12: Likelihood of Recommendation (0-10)
**Score: 7/10**

I'd recommend this to other analytical bettors who prioritize data over entertainment. For casual bettors or those who want the thrill component, I'd suggest they might find it boring. It's a solid B+ for my specific use case.
