## VARIANT 1

**Q1: What's your overall feeling when looking at this interface?**
Clean and professional. It's got that serious, established vibe - reminds me of checking stats on ESPN. The dark background with bright text feels like I'm looking at a dashboard built for business, not play. Makes me feel focused.

**Q2: Which specific part of the screen drives that feeling most?**
The deep navy/charcoal background. It's doing the heavy lifting here. The bright text pops against it, but it's that dark base that sets the tone - makes everything feel weighty and important. The contrast is sharp, which I need when I'm scanning odds quickly.

**Q3: Does it feel more energizing or more pressuring?**
Energizing, but in a controlled way. It's not hyping me up like I'm at a stadium, but it's keeping me alert. The high contrast wakes my brain up without making me feel rushed. Good for those marathon betting sessions during playoffs.

**Q4: Do you feel more in control, or does the app feel more in control?**
I'm in control here. The layout is straightforward, the colors aren't manipulating me. It feels like I'm using a tool I chose, not being guided through someone else's funhouse. That matters when real money's on the line.

**Q5: What level of restraint do you feel from the design? (1=none, 10=maximum)**
7/10. It's not stopping me from doing anything, but it's definitely not encouraging impulse bets either. The seriousness of the color scheme makes me pause and think - which is actually what I want when I'm about to drop money on a game.

**Q6: What specific design elements create that level of restraint?**
That dark background is the main restraint mechanism. It's somber, makes me take things seriously. There's no bright reds or golds screaming "BET NOW!" The whole thing says "make smart decisions" rather than "feel the excitement!"

**Q7: How visually fatigued would you feel after 20-30 minutes? (1=none, 10=maximum)**
4/10. The high contrast keeps things readable, but after half an hour of staring at this during a live game, my eyes would start feeling the strain. That bright-on-dark contrast is crisp but demanding. Would probably need a break between halves.

**Q8: What age range does this design seem targeted toward?**
28-45. This is for people who've been around the block, who have disposable income and take their bets seriously. Not for college kids looking for a thrill. It's mature without being old.

**Q9: What kind of betting behavior does this seem designed to encourage?**
Strategic, calculated betting. The design says "analyze the odds, make informed decisions, track your performance." It's built for people who view betting as skill-based, not pure luck. It's not pushing me toward parlays or crazy long shots.

**Q10: How likely would you be to return to this app? (1=never, 10=definitely)**
8/10. This feels reliable and professional. I'd trust it with my money. The design tells me they're focused on functionality over flash, which means they're probably focused on fair odds and smooth payouts too. I'd be back.

**Q11: Does it feel more like entertainment or more like a tool?**
Definitely a tool. This is a calculator, not a game. It's for serious bettors who want information presented clearly so they can make smart plays. The fun comes from winning, not from the app itself.

**Q12: Does it feel confident/established or experimental/in-your-face?**
Confident and established. This feels like a platform that's been around, that knows what it's doing. It's not trying to prove anything or grab attention with wild design choices. It's secure in its identity.
