# UX Color System Testing - Betting App
## User Persona: Liam, 31, Cross-Entertainment Engager

**Test Date:** December 12, 2025
**Testing Duration:** 65 minutes
**Environment:** MacBook Pro, Evening session after work

---

## About Me
I'm Liam, 31 years old, and I use a lot of entertainment apps daily - Netflix, Spotify, Steam, and various gaming platforms. I expect all my apps to feel modern, seamless, and polished. When I'm looking at a betting app, I'm comparing it to the design quality of my other entertainment platforms. If it doesn't measure up, I'm probably not sticking around.

---

## Variant 1 - Pink/Purple Gradient System

### Q1: First Impression (0-10)
**Rating: 3/10**

Honestly? This feels like a dating app, not a betting platform. The pink and purple gradient screams "romance app" to me. When I open this, my first thought is "Did I accidentally click the wrong app?" It's way too soft and feminine for what should be an energetic, competitive environment.

### Q2: Color Contrast & Readability (0-10)
**Rating: 5/10**

The white text on the gradient backgrounds is okay, but not great. Some areas have decent contrast, but when the gradient transitions through lighter pink sections, I'm squinting. The balance information at the top is particularly hard to read. For something as important as my money, I need crystal-clear readability.

### Q3: Visual Hierarchy (0-10)
**Rating: 4/10**

Everything kind of blends together with these pastel colors. The action buttons don't pop enough. When I'm used to Netflix's bold red or Spotify's vibrant green, this feels washed out. I can't quickly identify what's important versus what's secondary information.

### Q4: Emotional Response (0-10)
**Rating: 2/10**

This makes me feel... calm? Relaxed? That's the opposite of what I want from a betting app. I want excitement, energy, that rush. This feels like a meditation app. The colors are making me think of sunset yoga sessions, not competitive sports betting.

### Q5: Trust & Credibility (0-10)
**Rating: 3/10**

The pastel pink/purple combination actually hurts credibility for me. It doesn't feel serious or professional. Major finance and betting apps use strong, confident colors. This looks like it was designed for a teenage social media app. I'd question if this platform is legitimate.

### Q6: Modern vs Outdated (0-10)
**Rating: 4/10**

The gradient technique is modern, I'll give it that. But the color choice feels like a 2018 Instagram filter, not 2025 app design. It's trying to be trendy but missing the mark. Modern apps in the entertainment space use bold, confident color choices.

### Q7: Visual Comfort (0-10)
**Rating: 6/10**

Actually, it's pretty comfortable to look at - maybe too comfortable. It's easy on the eyes for extended viewing, but that's because there's no visual energy. It's like watching paint dry. Comfortable, yes, but boring.

### Q8: Information Scannability (0-10)
**Rating: 4/10**

The soft colors make it hard to quickly scan for information. When I'm checking odds or my bets, I need instant clarity. This requires more focus than it should. Compare this to how easily I can scan through Spotify playlists or Netflix categories - this is much harder.

### Q9: Alignment with Betting Context (0-10)
**Rating: 2/10**

This is completely misaligned with betting. Betting should feel dynamic, competitive, maybe even slightly aggressive in a fun way. This feels like a wellness app. The colors communicate the wrong emotion entirely.

### Q10: Comparison to Top Entertainment Apps (0-10)
**Rating: 3/10**

Netflix uses bold reds and blacks. Spotify has vibrant green. Gaming platforms use deep blues and energetic oranges. This pink/purple scheme doesn't compete with any of them. It looks amateur compared to my other apps.

### Q11: Likelihood to Use Long-term (0-10)
**Rating: 2/10**

I'd probably try it once out of curiosity, but I wouldn't stick around. Every time I opened the app, I'd be reminded of how it doesn't fit my expectation of what a betting platform should be. I'd migrate to something that feels more appropriate.

### Q12: Overall UX Quality (0-10)
**Rating: 3/10**

Technically functional, but emotionally and contextually wrong. The design execution is okay, but the fundamental color choice is a critical mistake. It's like putting a romantic soundtrack on an action movie - it just doesn't work.
