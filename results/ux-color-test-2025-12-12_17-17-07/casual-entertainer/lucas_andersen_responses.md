# UX Color System Testing - Casual Entertainer Persona
## Lucas Andersen - 23-year-old Bartender from Odense, Denmark

Testing Date: 2025-12-12
Tester Profile: Casual sports bettor, mobile-first user, values fun and social features
Language: Danish/English

---

## VARIATION 1 - Dark Blue/Teal Base

### Q1: First Impression
Super clean, professional but not boring. The dark blue reminds me a bit of the big betting sites like Betfair. It feels serious though - like something I'd use during a big match. Nothing really jumps out at me as "fun."

### Q2: Color Appeal
The teal accents are nice, very modern. But honestly? I'd want something a bit more energetic when I'm placing a casual bet during my break at the bar. This feels a bit corporate, like something my accountant would use. 6/10 for my vibe.

### Q3: Button Visibility
The buttons stand out fine - you can definitely see where to tap. The call-to-action colors are clear. No issues finding what I need to click.

### Q4: Text Readability
White text on dark background is good for late-night scrolling when I'm betting after my shift. No strain on the eyes. Perfect for that.

### Q5: Visual Hierarchy
Everything is organized, but it doesn't really guide me to "bet now!" The hierarchy is there, but it's calm. Maybe too calm?

### Q6: Brand Personality
Professional, trustworthy. But where's the excitement? Where's the "let's go!" feeling? Feels more like a bank app than a betting app.

### Q7: Accessibility
Easy to use. Clear contrasts. I could navigate this with my eyes closed, which is good when I'm behind the bar juggling drinks and phones.

### Q8: Emotional Response
Neutral. Safe. A bit boring for casual betting. Doesn't make me want to place a bet. More like "okay, I can use this" than "hell yeah, let's do this!"

### Q9: Sports Context Relevance
Works fine for sports betting, but doesn't feel "sporty" to me. Could be any app really. No energy, no atmosphere of a stadium or live action.

### Q10: Mobile Experience
Looks solid on mobile. Good spacing, not cramped. I could definitely use this one-handed while serving drinks.

### Q11: Social Features Integration
Hard to see any social elements standing out here. If there's community features, they're not visually prominent. As someone who likes sharing bets with mates, this feels a bit lonely.

### Q12: Likelihood to Use (1-10)
**6/10** - I'd use it, it works, but I wouldn't be excited about it. Reliable but forgettable.

## COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS - Q13: Best Overall

**#9 (Warm Red/Energetic) WINS** - 9/10

This is my favorite for being a casual entertainer. Here's why:

1. **Energy + Accessibility Balance**: It has the excitement I need for betting without stressing me out. Unlike pure red (#8), it doesn't feel aggressive.

2. **Social-Friendly**: The warm color naturally invites sharing and social interaction. Perfect for my use case of betting with mates.

3. **Mobile Perfection**: As someone who bets during bar shifts, I need something that works on mobile. #9 is gorgeous and functional on small screens.

4. **Emotional Appeal**: It makes me feel excited and happy, not just rushed. This is the difference between a betting app I love and a betting app I tolerate.

5. **Versatility**: Works for casual bets, live betting, and sharing moments with friends. Not too intense, not too boring.

**Runner-ups**:
- #3 (Vibrant Purple) - 8.5/10 - Almost tied, but the purple is slightly less accessible and less warm
- #5 (Coral/Orange) - 8/10 - Super friendly but maybe not quite enough energy for the thrill I seek

## Final Recommendations

**For Yes Games targeting casual entertainers like me:**

1. **Primary Color System**: Adopt #9 (Warm Red/Energetic) as main
   - Use it for primary call-to-actions and key features
   - It's exciting but not aggressive
   - Socially inviting
   - Works beautifully on mobile

2. **Secondary/Accent Colors**: Layer in #5 (Coral) for secondary elements
   - Provides warmth and approachability
   - Works well with the warm red
   - Better contrast for accessibility
   - Good for social/sharing features

3. **Dark Mode Option**: Definitely offer #4 (Dark Purple)
   - Essential for evening users like me
   - Protects eyes during late-night sessions
   - Feel premium and sophisticated

4. **Avoid**:
   - #1 (Dark Blue) - Too corporate, not exciting enough
   - #6 (Bright Green) - Too intense, causes eye strain
   - Pure accessibility focus without energy - I won't use it if it's boring

**Why this matters**: As a 23-year-old bartender who bets casually during my breaks and evenings, I need an app that understands my psychology. I want excitement and the psychological rush of betting, but I also want to feel like the app is friendly and designed for people like me, not just extraction machines. The warmth of #9 combined with the accessibility and social features suggested by the coral accents would make me loyal to this app.

The golden premium option (#10) is beautiful for special occasions, but #9 is what I'd use every day.
