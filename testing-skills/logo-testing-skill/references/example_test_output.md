# Example Test Output - Logo Testing

This is a sample of what a completed logo test report looks like.

## Test Configuration
- **Persona tested:** The Trust Seeker (Age 47, Aarhus)
- **Context:** Evaluating logo as app icon (120x120px)
- **Device:** iPhone 14
- **Rounds per persona:** 10
- **Logos tested:**
  - **Logo A:** Minimalist "Y" mark in gradient (blue→green)
  - **Logo B:** Traditional playing card + dice icon in red
  - **Logo C:** Modern "Yes" wordmark in bold sans-serif
  - **Logo D:** Abstract geometric shape in vibrant colors
- **Visual Assets Provided:** PNG files at 1024x1024px (full color, app icon versions, monochrome)
- **Date conducted:** November 3, 2025

## Quantitative Results

### Q1: First Impression (Which makes strongest positive first impression?)
The Trust Seeker:
- Logo C ("Yes" wordmark): 1/10 (10%)
- **Logo B (Playing card + dice)**: 7/10 (70%)
- Logo A (Minimalist "Y"): 2/10 (20%)
- Logo D (Abstract geometric): 0/10 (0%)

### Q4: Trust & Quality ⭐ MOST CRITICAL (Which suggests most trustworthy platform?)
The Trust Seeker:
- Logo C ("Yes" wordmark): 2/10 (20%)
- **Logo B (Playing card + dice)**: 8/10 (80%)
- Logo A (Minimalist "Y"): 0/10 (0%)
- Logo D (Abstract geometric): 0/10 (0%)

### Q5: Recognition (Which would you most easily recognize again?)
The Trust Seeker:
- Logo C ("Yes" wordmark): 3/10 (30%)
- **Logo B (Playing card + dice)**: 6/10 (60%)
- Logo A (Minimalist "Y"): 1/10 (10%)
- Logo D (Abstract geometric): 0/10 (0%)

### Preference-Trust-Memory Triangle Analysis
**Strong Alignment:** Logo B wins all three key metrics
- **Preference (Q1):** 70%
- **Trust (Q4):** 80% ⭐
- **Memory (Q5):** 60%

**Winner:** Logo B (Playing card + dice) - Clear consensus across all dimensions

## Key Learnings

### Trust Beats Trendy for Gambling
Modern, minimalist design (Logo A) had some aesthetic appeal but **zero trust**.
- **Implication:** In gambling, trust is currency. Don't sacrifice it for design trends.

### Industry Symbols Matter
Playing cards and dice are **visual shorthand for legitimate gambling**:
- Users instantly recognize and trust these symbols
- Attempting to "reinvent" gambling visuals backfires
- **Implication:** Use established visual language of your industry

### The Trust Gap is Deadly
Logo A showed the danger of preference without trust:
- Users might click/download based on visual appeal
- But immediately distrust once they're asked to deposit money
- **Result:** High acquisition cost, zero retention

### Scalability is Critical
Logo must work from 16px favicon to billboard:
- Simple, clear symbols (Logo B) scale perfectly
- Complex or abstract designs (Logo D) become illegible
- **Implication:** Test at smallest size first - if it fails at 16px, it fails

### Age Matters for Logo Testing
Trust Seeker (age 47) heavily weighted trust and familiarity:
- Traditional symbols = safe
- Modern abstractions = suspicious
- **Implication:** Test with your highest-value demographic (usually 35+)

### The Preference-Trust-Memory Triangle Reveals Everything
All three metrics must align for logo success:
- ✅ Logo B: Strong across all three = winner
- ❌ Logo A: Preference without trust = failure
- ❌ Logo C: Weak across all three = forgotten
- ❌ Logo D: Zero everywhere = catastrophe

**Implication:** Don't just test "which do you like?" - measure trust and memory too.
